Autobiography of Elder Esther Wambui Muiruri

BIRTH

Elder Esther Wambui Muiruri was born in 1960 to the late Mr. Benson Boro Kimani and Mrs. Martha Muthoni Boro at Giathieko Village, Kiambu Sub-County, Kiambu County.

EDUCATION

Her scholastic journey began at Kanjai Primary in 1968, where she later sat for her C.P.E. examination in 1975. She then proceeded to Central Girls' School, Mombasa, before joining Muguga High School, where she did her junior exams.

MARRIAGE

In 1989, Elder Esther Muiruri was united in holy matrimony with the late Jamie Ayub Muiruri. Their marriage was a testimony of God's grace, blessed with three children, four grandchildren, and one great-grandchild.

CHURCH & LEADERSHIP

CHURCH

Elder Esther was born and brought up in a Christian Faith Family. She joined P.C.E.A Kanjai Church in her early childhood, later worshipped at P.C.E.A St. Margaret Mombasa and P.C.E.A Ayub Kinyua, before returning to her home church at P.C.E.A Kanjai. In 1989, she was ordained as a member of the Woman's Guild.

A) ORDINATION TO ELDERSHIP

She was ordained as a church elder in the year 2000 by Rev. Muniu Muchai at P.C.E.A Kiambururu Parish, Kambui Presbytery. Since then, she has faithfully served in the following districts:

  • Kianjogu
  • Kanjai Center
  • Karweti
  • Githunguri (her last district of service)
  • Giachumi
  • Gicunji

B) PATRONAGE

She has served as a patron for the Boys and Girls Brigade.

C) LEADERSHIP

  • Church Deacon for six years
  • L.C.C Secretary for six years
  • Woman's Guild Treasurer for three years

D) T.E.E

In 2009, she completed a Diploma in Theological Education by Extension and pursued Brigade Basic Course I.

VOTE OF THANKS

With a heart overflowing in praise, Elder Esther wishes to glorify the Almighty Father in heaven, who has sustained her through a full jubilee season of service. In this jubilant jubilee season, the Lord has been her ever present help, her guide and the song of her heart.

She joyfully celebrates her family, where love, sacrifice and steadfast support have been the wind beneath her wings throughout this sacred journey.

She warmly honours the presbytery fraternity, her fellow Elders and the Parish Minister, whose prayers, encouragement and fellowship have refreshed her soul. Their leadership, companionship and partnership in the ministry have been a source of strength and inspiration.
